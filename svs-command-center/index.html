🎓 Course & academy
Learning centre · business + photography
📘 Business — idea to first shoot
The one idea: someone pays you to capture a moment well and hand it back beautifully. Everything else just serves that. Most beginners fail not from bad photos, but from never finishing the loop once: book → shoot → deliver → get paid. Do it once, then repeat a little better.
Lesson 1
Decide what you sell — and to whom
A restaurant with 100 dishes confuses everyone. A short menu says "we know what we're good at."
Pick 2–3 things you shoot and who they're for. Narrow = easy to recommend and book.
Do finish: "I take ___ photos for ___ in ___."
Don't be everything to everyone day one.
Lesson 2
Make it real — the leak-proof bucket
Money is water. One bucket leaks; labelled buckets give every drop a home.
Register, separate accounts, split every payment the day it lands: 50/20/15/15.
Do split immediately; never touch the tax bucket.
Don't mix business and personal money.
Lesson 3
Your face to the world
A brand is a face people learn to recognise — keep it the same.
One name, clean logo, one or two colours/fonts, reused everywhere.
Do keep one look across everything.
Don't redesign weekly or copy five photographers.
Lesson 4
Gear — buy only what the next job needs
A chef doesn't buy 20 knives before opening.
Camera, one lens, cards, backup drive. Add lights only when a booked job needs them. Cap at 4.
Do master one camera + one lens.
Don't spend savings "to be ready." Work books clients.
Lesson 5
Price without fear
A price is a promise of value, not an apology.
Three packages, clear inclusions. No deposit, no booking — 50% up front, balance before delivery.
Do take the deposit before you commit.
Don't drop price from nerves or shoot before it clears.
Lesson 6
A simple system to run it
A pilot uses a checklist, not memory.
Track five things: leads → bookings → shoots → deliveries → money. Log same day; review weekly. (This dashboard is that system.)
Do a 15-min review each week.
Don't keep it in your head.
Lesson 7
Protect yourself
A seatbelt is boring until the day it isn't.
One-page contract. 3-2-1 backup every shoot. Insurance + tax early.
Do back up every shoot; contract even with friends.
Don't deliver without payment or keep one copy.
Lesson 8
Get your first client (no ads)
Your first customers already trust you — start with the warm circle.
Tell everyone with one offer, post consistently (two platforms), pitch local businesses.
Do message contacts, pitch 3, post 2×/week.
Don't wait to be found or buy ads early.
Lesson 9
Before the shoot
A calm shoot is one you rehearsed the night before.
Confirm details, charge batteries, format cards, check light, write a shot list.
Do confirm the day before; test gear the night before.
Don't arrive with one battery and no plan.
Lesson 10
The shoot day
You're the calm host, not a nervous guest.
Safe shots first, then creative. Direct gently, glance at your screen, don't hide behind it.
Do lock the essential shots early.
Don't only experiment and forget the basics.
Lesson 11
After — edit, deliver, get paid, follow up
The meal isn't done until it's plated and served.
Back up, cull, edit consistently, deliver on time after full payment, then ask for a review + referral.
Do deliver on time; ask for referrals.
Don't deliver late, hand over RAWs, or vanish.
Lesson 12
Improve & raise prices
Each batch of jobs unlocks the next level.
After ~10 jobs, raise prices 15–20% and learn one new skill. Reinvest the 20% bucket.
Do track your count; raise on schedule.
Don't stay cheap forever or stop learning.
First 30 days
A simple plan
Week 1 — Make it real. Menu, register, accounts, split, name + logo.Week 2 — Get ready. Packages + contract, gallery + backup, practise.Week 3 — Create demand. Tell your circle, pitch 3, post, set up your tracker.Week 4 — Finish the loop. Book, prep, shoot, deliver, get paid — then review.
